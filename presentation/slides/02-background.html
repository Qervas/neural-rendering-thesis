Why NeRF Handles Reflections

The network takes viewing direction as input!

Same 3D point can return different colors depending on where you look from.

This models reflections naturally.

A shiny surface looks different from different angles — NeRF learns that!

Input:

(x, y, z) + (θ, φ)

position + direction

↓

Output:

(R, G, B) + σ

color + density

Think of It Like This...

Imagine your scene is made of millions of tiny colored clouds

Each "cloud" (Gaussian) has:

  • A position in 3D
  • A shape (round, stretched, etc.)
  • A color
  • Transparency

To render: Project all clouds onto the screen, blend them together
